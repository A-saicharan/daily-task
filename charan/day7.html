About Rohit Sharma

Rohit Gurunath Sharma (born April 30, 1987) is an iconic Indian cricketer and former captain of the national team across all formats.
Nicknamed the "Hitman," he is widely celebrated as one of the greatest limited-overs batters in history,
holding the world record for the highest individual ODI score (264) and being the only player with three ODI double-centuries.

  • He is the first player to score 200 in ODIs.
  • He has scored 36 centuries in ODIs, the most by any Indian player.
  • He has scored 7 centuries in T20Is, the most by any player in this format.
  • He has won the ICC Cricket World Cup twice (2011 and 2015).
  • He has won the ICC Champions Trophy twice (2002 and 2013).
  • He has been awarded the prestigious Arjuna Award and Padma Shri for his contributions to Indian cricket.

T20 Career

Rohit Sharma retired from T20 International (T20I) cricket in June 2024 as the format's premier six-hitter and a T20 World Cup-winning captain.
In 159 T20I matches, he amassed 4,231 runs at a strike rate of 140.89, including five centuries and 32 half-centuries.

  • T20 World Cup Triumph:
    He led the India National Cricket Team to their second ICC Men's T20 World Cup title in June 2024 before announcing his retirement from T20Is.
  • Six-Hitting Record:
    He holds the record for the most sixes by an Indian in the IPL and is one of only a few players globally to blast over 500 sixes across his overall T20 career.
  • IPL Legacy:
    As a cornerstone for the Mumbai Indians, he won five IPL titles as captain (2013, 2015, 2017, 2019, 2020) and one with the Deccan Chargers. Across his IPL career, he has accumulated over 7,300 runs.
